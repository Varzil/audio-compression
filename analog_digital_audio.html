Understanding Digital Audio

Sampling Rate: How many times per second we measure the analog signal. CD quality uses 44,100 samples/second (44.1 kHz). Higher = more accurate representation.

Bit Depth: How many possible amplitude levels each sample can have. 16-bit = 65,536 levels. Higher = more dynamic range and less quantization noise.

Audio File Formats Explained

MP3

LOSSY
  • Full name: MPEG-1 Audio Layer 3
  • Compression: 10:1 typical
  • How it works: Uses psychoacoustic models to remove sounds humans can't hear well
  • Removes: Quiet sounds masked by loud ones, frequencies outside hearing range
  • Best for: Music libraries, portable players
  • Quality: 128-320 kbps

AAC

LOSSY
  • Full name: Advanced Audio Coding
  • Compression: Better than MP3 at same bitrate
  • How it works: More advanced psychoacoustic model + better encoding
  • Advantages: Better quality than MP3 at lower bitrates
  • Best for: Streaming, Apple devices, YouTube
  • Used by: Apple Music, YouTube, Netflix

FLAC

LOSSLESS
  • Full name: Free Lossless Audio Codec
  • Compression: 2:1 typical (50-70% original size)
  • How it works: Like ZIP for audio - finds patterns and redundancy
  • Quality: Bit-perfect, identical to original
  • Best for: Archiving, audiophiles
  • Drawback: Larger files than MP3/AAC

üî¨ Compression Techniques Used

Lossy Compression (MP3, AAC):

  • Psychoacoustic Masking: Remove quiet sounds that occur at the same time as loud sounds
  • Frequency Masking: Remove frequencies close to louder ones
  • Temporal Masking: Remove sounds right before/after loud sounds
  • Huffman Coding: Use shorter codes for common patterns
  • MDCT: Modified Discrete Cosine Transform for frequency analysis

Lossless Compression (FLAC):

  • Linear Prediction: Predict next sample based on previous ones, store only the difference
  • Rice Coding: Efficient encoding of the prediction residuals
  • Run-Length Encoding: Compress sequences of similar values
  • No data loss: Can perfectly reconstruct original audio

üìà Real-World Comparison

Original WAV file: 50 MB (3 minutes, CD quality)

FLAC: ~25-35 MB (lossless - sounds identical)

AAC 256 kbps: ~6 MB (nearly indistinguishable for most listeners)

MP3 320 kbps: ~7 MB (high quality)

MP3 128 kbps: ~3 MB (noticeable quality loss)
